# Dutch Idioms & Expressions — the grounding bank for `idiom` posts

The source bank for the **`idiom`** Instagram post type. An idiom is the hardest thing to learn and the
most satisfying to "get", because the literal words hide the meaning. One post teaches a **set** of related
idioms (one card each) or goes deep on **one**. Each card carries four things, and the rendered
`ConceptGuide` carousel (variant: `idiom`) is built around them:

1. **the idiom** (NL), 2. the **literal** translation (the funny part), 3. the **real meaning**,
4. a short **example** in use (NL + EN).

> **The A1 carve-out lives here most of all.** `dutch-level-guide.md` flags idioms as above A1 by design
> (it even names `de kat uit de boom kijken` as a red flag). That is fine for this type, under the
> *Sanctioned exceptions* rule: the idiom is the advanced payload, so it is allowed, **but** the literal
> line, the meaning line, and the example's surrounding words all stay A1, and every line is glossed in
> English. Never publish a bare untranslated idiom. The whole format is "here is the strange literal, here
> is what it really means".

---

## Why idioms post well (for the intro slide)

The literal translation is the hook: "to see through the fingers", "now the monkey comes out of the
sleeve". It is funny, it is shareable, and the reveal (what it actually means) is the payload. Idioms also
signal real fluency, so learners save them. Lead with the literal, reveal the meaning, give one example.

## Animal idioms (a ready post: "5 Dutch animal idioms")

- **de kat uit de boom kijken** — *to look the cat out of the tree* — to wait and see before you act, hold
  back. · `Hij kijkt eerst de kat uit de boom.` — He waits to see how things go first.
- **nu komt de aap uit de mouw** — *now the monkey comes out of the sleeve* — now the hidden truth or real
  motive comes out. · `Aha, nu komt de aap uit de mouw.` — Aha, now the truth comes out.
- **een gegeven paard niet in de bek kijken** — *don't look a given horse in the mouth* — don't be
  ungrateful for a gift. · `Het is niet perfect, maar je moet een gegeven paard niet in de bek kijken.`
- **ergens geen kaas van gegeten hebben** — *to have eaten no cheese of something* — to know nothing about
  a subject. · `Ik heb er geen kaas van gegeten.` — I know nothing about it.
- **als de kippen erbij zijn** — *to be there like the chickens* — to be very quick to react. ·
  `Hij is er als de kippen bij.` — He jumps on it right away.

## Body idioms (a ready post: "Dutch idioms with body parts")

- **iets onder de knie hebben** — *to have something under the knee* — to have mastered it. ·
  `Ik heb de uitspraak nog niet onder de knie.` — I haven't mastered the pronunciation yet.
- **de draad kwijt zijn** — *to have lost the thread* — to have lost track of what you were saying. ·
  `Sorry, ik ben de draad kwijt.` — Sorry, I lost my train of thought.
- **iets uit je hoofd leren** — *to learn something out of your head* — to memorize. ·
  `Ik leer de woorden uit mijn hoofd.` — I memorize the words.
- **je hart luchten** — *to air your heart* — to get something off your chest. ·
  `Ze wilde even haar hart luchten.` — She wanted to vent for a moment.

## Food idioms (a ready post: "Dutch idioms about food")

- **een appeltje voor de dorst** — *a little apple for the thirst* — savings for a rainy day. ·
  `Dat geld is een appeltje voor de dorst.` — That money is for a rainy day.
- **ergens een broodje van bakken** — skip if unsure; verify before use.
- **helaas pindakaas** — *unfortunately peanut butter* — a playful "oh well, too bad" (it rhymes). ·
  `Geen kaartjes meer? Helaas pindakaas.` — No tickets left? Oh well.
- **met de gebakken peren zitten** — *to sit with the baked pears* — to be left with the mess / the
  consequences. · `Nu zit ik met de gebakken peren.` — Now I am stuck with the fallout.

## Everyday idioms (a ready post: "Dutch idioms you'll actually hear")

- **door de vingers zien** — *to see through the fingers* — to turn a blind eye, let it slide. ·
  `De docent zag de fout door de vingers.` — The teacher let the mistake slide.
- **met de deur in huis vallen** — *to fall into the house with the door* — to get straight to the point. ·
  `Ik val maar met de deur in huis.` — Let me get straight to the point.
- **de knoop doorhakken** — *to chop through the knot* — to finally make a decision. ·
  `We moeten de knoop doorhakken.` — We have to make the call.
- **de puntjes op de i zetten** — *to put the dots on the i* — to perfect the final details. ·
  `Even de puntjes op de i zetten.` — Just adding the finishing touches.
- **het regent pijpenstelen** — *it is raining pipe stems* — it is pouring. ·
  `Neem een jas mee, het regent pijpenstelen.` — Bring a coat, it is pouring.

## How an `idiom` post uses this bank

- **A set per post** (5 to 6 cards) grouped by a theme (animals, body, food, everyday), or **one idiom
  deep** (literal → meaning → two examples → when you'd say it).
- Card order on each slide: idiom (hero) → literal (the funny line) → meaning → one example pair.
- Lead the cover with the most surprising literal of the set ("now the monkey comes out of the sleeve").
- Keep the meaning line and the example simple and glossed. The idiom is the only above-A1 element.
- Caption line 1 = a searchable keyword ("Dutch idioms", "Dutch expressions and meanings").
- Pin a comment that pulls a reply ("which one is your favourite?" or "which made no sense at first?").
