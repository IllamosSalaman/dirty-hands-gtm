# Sound More Dutch тАФ the grounding bank for `small-words` posts

The source bank for the **`small-words`** Instagram post type. The angle: **sounding** Dutch is mostly the
tiny words you sprinkle into a sentence, not big vocabulary. One post teaches a **set** of these small
reaction, tag, filler, and intensifier words (one card each: the word, what it does, one example pair),
under a cover like "5 words that make you sound Dutch". The rendered `ConceptGuide` carousel (variant:
`small-words`) expects a set of short word-cards.

> **Relation to `modal-particles.md`.** Some words appear in both (`nou`, `toch`, `even`, `gewoon`,
> `hoor`). The framing is different and that is the point: `modal-particles.md` is the *grammar of tone*
> (how a particle bends a sentence, taught one particle deep). This file is the *conversational layer* (the
> little reaction and filler words you add to sound like a local, taught a handful wide). A `particle` post
> goes deep on one; a `small-words` post goes wide on several. Keep examples **A1** and always glossed
> (`dutch-level-guide.md`); these little words are a sanctioned A2-feel exception.

## Why this works (for the intro slide)

You can have correct Dutch and still sound like a textbook. What makes you sound Dutch is the small stuff:
the `h├и?` on the end, the `lekker` in front, the `precies!` when you agree. These words carry almost no
information and almost all the personality. Learn five and drop them in, and people stop switching to
English on you.

## Agreement and reaction (say these instead of "ja")

- **`zeker`** тАФ definitely / for sure. `Zeker!` тАФ Definitely! ┬╖ `Dat is zeker waar.` тАФ That is definitely true.
- **`precies`** тАФ exactly. `Precies!` тАФ Exactly! ┬╖ `Ja, precies dat.` тАФ Yes, exactly that.
- **`inderdaad`** тАФ indeed / that's right. `Inderdaad.` тАФ That's right. ┬╖ `Het is inderdaad laat.` тАФ It is indeed late.
- **`echt?`** тАФ really? `Echt?` тАФ Really? ┬╖ `Echt waar?` тАФ Is that true?
- **`klopt`** тАФ that's correct / right. `Dat klopt.` тАФ That's right. ┬╖ `Klopt!` тАФ Correct!

Tip: a one-word reaction (`Precies!`, `Zeker!`, `Klopt!`) sounds far more native than a plain `Ja`.

## Tags you stick on the end

- **`h├и?`** тАФ right? / eh? `Lekker weer, h├и?` тАФ Nice weather, right? ┬╖ `Gezellig, h├и?` тАФ Cosy, isn't it?
- **`toch?`** тАФ right? (seeking a yes). `Je komt toch?` тАФ You are coming, right?
- **`hoor`** тАФ reassuring, warm. `Het is goed hoor.` тАФ It is fine, really. ┬╖ `Geen probleem hoor.` тАФ No problem at all.
- **`joh`** тАФ friendly "man / hey". `Joh, doe rustig.` тАФ Hey, relax. ┬╖ `Welnee joh.` тАФ Oh no, don't worry.

Tip: `h├и?` and `hoor` turn a flat statement into a friendly one. `hoor` and `joh` only go at the end.

## Fillers and openers (buy yourself a second)

- **`nou`** тАФ well. `Nou, ik weet het niet.` тАФ Well, I am not sure. ┬╖ `Nou en?` тАФ So what?
- **`eigenlijk`** тАФ actually. `Eigenlijk wel.` тАФ Actually, yes. ┬╖ `Ik weet het eigenlijk niet.` тАФ I actually don't know.
- **`gewoon`** тАФ just / simply. `Het is gewoon zo.` тАФ That's just how it is. ┬╖ `Doe gewoon normaal.` тАФ Just act normal.
- **`even`** тАФ for a sec. `Wacht even.` тАФ Hold on a sec. ┬╖ `Ik kijk even.` тАФ Let me have a quick look.
- **`zeg`** тАФ hey / say. `Zeg, heb je even?` тАФ Hey, got a second?

Tip: `nou...` and `eigenlijk...` at the start of a reply sound natural and give you thinking time.

## Intensifiers (turn it up the Dutch way)

- **`lekker`** тАФ not only "tasty": nicely / comfortably. `Lekker rustig.` тАФ Nice and quiet. ┬╖ `Lekker weer.` тАФ Lovely weather. ┬╖ `Slaap lekker.` тАФ Sleep well.
- **`best`** тАФ quite / pretty. `Best lekker.` тАФ Pretty good. ┬╖ `Best wel druk.` тАФ Quite busy actually.
- **`helemaal`** тАФ totally / all the way. `Helemaal goed.` тАФ Totally fine. ┬╖ `Ik ben het helemaal vergeten.` тАФ I completely forgot.
- **`echt`** тАФ really (intensifier). `Echt lekker.` тАФ Really good. ┬╖ `Echt niet.` тАФ No way. / Definitely not.

Tip: `lekker` in front of almost anything (`lekker wandelen`, `lekker thuis`) is peak Dutch.

## The untranslatables (worth their own post)

- **`gezellig`** тАФ cosy + convivial + nice-together, all at once. `Wat gezellig!` тАФ How nice (and cosy)! ┬╖ `Het was heel gezellig.` тАФ It was really lovely.
- **`lekker`** (again) тАФ used for comfort and wellbeing, not just food. `Lekker belangrijk.` тАФ Yeah, "important" (sarcastic). `Ga lekker zitten.` тАФ Make yourself comfortable.

Tip: a post on `gezellig` alone does well: name the feeling, give three settings, admit it has no English word.

## How a `small-words` post uses this bank

- **A set per post**, usually 5 to 7 cards: cover ("words that make you sound Dutch"), one card per word
  (word + one-line meaning + one example pair), CTA. Group by section above (e.g. "5 ways to agree", "4
  little tags Dutch people add", "say this instead of ja").
- Pick words that **share a job** so the post has a spine, not a random list.
- A single untranslatable (`gezellig`, `lekker`) can also carry a whole post (more like a `particle` post
  in shape).
- Keep examples A1 and glossed; the small word is the payload, the sentence around it stays simple.
- Caption line 1 = a searchable keyword ("how to sound more Dutch", "Dutch filler words").
- Pin a comment that pulls a reply ("which one will you use first?").
