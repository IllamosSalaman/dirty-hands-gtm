## Section 2 — Passing the exam is not the same as keeping the Dutch (THESIS / spine)
- **H2:** Passing the exam is not the same as keeping the Dutch
- **Word target:** 300
- **Key argument:** The real fork in the market is how a course defines "done." Most courses count you finished when you complete the exercises (trains recognition: you can pick the right answer). A few count you finished when you can use the language unprompted across all four skills (trains production). Recognition fades fast after the exam; production sticks. The exam itself tests both recognition and production — Speaking and Writing require producing Dutch — which is exactly why a recognition-only prep can get you through and still leave you unable to use Dutch a year later.
