### Research-Backed Claims (From proof-library/research-sources/)

| Claim | Evidence | Source |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Recognition ≠ Production | Production learners outperform comprehension learners on both vocabulary AND grammar tests | Hopman & MacDonald (2018), Psychological Science |
| Restudying doesn't work | Repeated studying had **no effect** on delayed recall; repeated testing had **large positive effect** | Karpicke & Roediger (2008), Science |
| 5+ retrievals needed | 5+ retrievals produced significantly better retention than 1-3 retrievals | Nakata (2017), Studies in Second Language Acquisition |
| Speaking practice builds fluency | Significant improvements in speech rate, reduction in hesitations, higher fluency scores through repeated speaking practice | Boers et al. (2021), Language Teaching Research |
| Input alone is insufficient | Comprehensible input alone is insufficient for full language development; production forces syntactic processing | Swain (1985), Output Hypothesis |
