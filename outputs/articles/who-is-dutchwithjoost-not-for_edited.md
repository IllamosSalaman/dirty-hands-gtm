# Who Is Dutchwithjoost NOT For?

Dutchwithjoost is a self-paced A2 inburgering course focused on production-based learning. It's not for everyone: if you need B1 level, are assigned a course by your municipality, need classroom accountability, have zero budget, or just want to pass fast without learning to speak, other options fit better. This guide helps you decide before you spend €399.

## Why We're Writing This (And Why Most Courses Won't)

Most Dutch courses want every sale. We'd rather have students who succeed than customers who buy and quit.

Here's why: a bad-fit student hurts everyone. They don't get results because the course wasn't built for their situation. They leave frustrated. They take support time from people we can actually help. And we'd rather save you €399 than watch you struggle with something that was never right for you.

This is unusual. Most courses hide their limitations. They'll take your money and let you figure out the mismatch yourself. We think that's backwards. If we're honest about [who we're built for](INTERNAL_LINK: inburgering-course-worth-it), we get students who actually finish. And you get clarity before you commit.

So here are the five situations where Dutchwithjoost is not the right choice.

## You Need B1 Level (Not A2)

If your DUO letter requires B1 level, Dutchwithjoost cannot help you. We only offer A2.

This affects people who fall under the 2021 Civic Integration Act (Wet inburgering 2021) and received their BSN after January 1, 2022. Under this law, family migrants and other migrants typically need B1 level, not A2.

**How to check your required level:**

- Look at your DUO letter. It specifies your required exam level.
- If you're unsure, contact DUO directly at inburgeren.nl.
- If your letter says B1, you need a B1 course.

**Who still needs A2:**

- People under the older 2013 Act (Wet inburgering 2013)
- Some asylum status holders on specific tracks
- People who started their integration before 2022

We plan to offer B1 in the future, but we don't have it now. If you need B1 today, look for courses that explicitly cover B1 inburgering. We'd rather you find the right course than buy ours and discover the mismatch later.

## You're Assigned a Course by Your Municipality

If you're an asylum status holder under the 2021 Act, your municipality assigns your integration course. You cannot choose your own.

This is a legal requirement, not a fit issue. Under the 2021 Act, the municipality pays for your course and your first two exam attempts are free. In exchange, you follow the course they assign.

This applies specifically to recognized refugees (statushouders) under the 2021 Act. Family migrants and other migrants under the same act still choose and pay for their own courses.

If your municipality assigned you a course, that's your path. Dutchwithjoost is not an option regardless of whether it would be a good fit.

## You Want a Classroom Experience

If you need external accountability to stay motivated, Dutchwithjoost is not the right fit. We're fully self-paced.

Some people need the structure of showing up at a fixed time every week. They need a human teacher who knows their name. They need classmates who create social pressure to prepare. That's not a weakness. It's a valid learning style.

**Signs you need a classroom course:**

- You've started and abandoned multiple self-paced courses
- You work better with external deadlines than internal motivation
- You want peer interaction as part of learning
- You value the human relationship with a teacher

**What classroom courses offer that we don't:**

- Fixed schedule that forces consistency
- In-person accountability
- Social learning environment
- Human teacher who adapts to your questions in real-time

If that's what you need, look at classroom options like Koentact, TaalBoost, or Language Corner. These courses cost more and require schedule commitment, but they provide accountability we don't offer. [Self-paced and classroom courses serve different needs](INTERNAL_LINK: self-paced-vs-classroom-dutch-courses).

What Dutchwithjoost offers instead: flexibility to learn whenever you have time, unlimited speaking practice without scheduling, and the ability to progress at your own pace. That works for self-motivated learners. It doesn't work for everyone.

## Your Budget Is Zero

Dutchwithjoost costs €399. If you have no budget at all, free resources exist.

**What €0 gets you:**

- Duolingo: Gamified vocabulary practice, good for habit building, not designed for inburgering
- YouTube: Free lessons and explanations, no structure or curriculum
- Library books: Grammar references and textbooks, no speaking practice
- DUO practice exams: Official example exams at inburgeren.nl, essential for any preparation

Free resources have real value. Duolingo builds habits. YouTube can explain grammar. Library books provide depth. DUO practice exams show you the actual exam format.

**What free resources don't provide:**

- Structured curriculum mapped to all five exam parts
- Speaking practice with feedback
- Progress tracking toward exam readiness
- KNM (Knowledge of Dutch Society) coverage

**If €399 is a stretch but not impossible:**

Family migrants and other migrants can borrow from DUO to cover course costs. The current interest rate (2026) is 2.29%. You can also borrow for exam fees. Check inburgeren.nl/en/paying-for-integration for details.

[The real cost of inburgering preparation](INTERNAL_LINK: inburgering-course-cost) depends on which path you choose. Free resources require more self-direction. Paid courses provide more structure. €399 for a complete course is mid-range for self-paced options.

## You Just Want to Pass Fast (And Don't Care About Speaking)

If your only goal is passing the exam as quickly as possible, memorization-based courses are faster. Dutchwithjoost is built for people who want to pass AND actually speak Dutch afterward.

Here's the trade-off, stated honestly:

**Memorization approach:**

- Learn to recognize correct answers
- Practice with example exams
- Optimize for passing the test
- Faster timeline to exam day
- Lower retention after the exam

**Production approach (what we do):**

- Learn by using vocabulary in conversation
- Lessons complete when you can produce the language, not just recognize it
- Takes longer
- Higher retention after the exam
- You can actually use Dutch when you're done

Research supports this trade-off. Karpicke and Roediger (2008) found that retrieval practice (producing information, not just recognizing it) leads to 40% better long-term retention than re-studying. [Production-based learning](INTERNAL_LINK: what-is-production-based-language-learning) is harder and slower, but it sticks.

Many expats preparing for inburgering report the same frustration: they passed the exam but freeze when they try to speak. They recognize Dutch but can't produce it. That's the recognition-production gap.

If you don't care about that gap, if you just need to check the box and move on, memorization courses exist. They work. People pass with them. InburgeringOnline is a well-established option for this approach. If speed is your priority, that approach will get you there faster.

Dutchwithjoost is built for people who want both: pass the exam AND be able to have a conversation afterward. That takes longer. If you're not willing to invest that time, we're not the right fit.
