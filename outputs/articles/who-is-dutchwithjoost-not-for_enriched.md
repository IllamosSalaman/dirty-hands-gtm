## Section 6: You Just Want to Pass Fast (And Don't Care About Speaking)

**Word target:** 250
**Key argument:** If your only goal is passing fast without retention, memorization courses may be faster. We're built for people who want to pass AND speak.

### Evidence Mapped

**Production vs. recognition research:**
- Swain's Output Hypothesis (1985): Production forces learners to process language more deeply than comprehension alone
- Karpicke & Roediger (2008): Retrieval practice produces 40% better long-term retention than re-study
- Production-based learning takes longer but produces durable results

**The recognition-production gap:**
- Pain point validated across community discussions: "I understand Dutch but freeze when speaking"
- Recognition is easier — listening, reading, multiple choice
- Production is harder — speaking, writing, generating language

**Our approach (honest):**
- We require production, not just recognition
- You must use vocabulary in conversation before it counts as learned
- This takes longer than pure memorization courses

**When memorization might be faster:**
- If you only care about passing, not speaking afterward
- If you have a very tight deadline and just need to check the box
- Memorization courses optimize for exam tricks, not language acquisition

**ICP disqualifier:**
- "Just wants to pass fast — Memorization courses are faster if retention doesn't matter"

**Evidence gaps:** None. Research sources and positioning provide strong backing.
