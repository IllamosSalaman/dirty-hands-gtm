# Product Documentation

This folder contains detailed product documentation for Dutchwithjoost. These documents explain **how the product works** at a conceptual level, providing the foundation for accurate, specific content creation.

## Documents

### messaging.md
The single source of truth for how to describe Dutchwithjoost in content. Contains:
- Approved one-sentence descriptions
- Core differentiator framing (production vs. memorization)
- How to describe lessons, Joost, and the learning experience
- Competitor positioning guidelines
- Claims we can and cannot make
- Approved vs. prohibited language

**Use this when:** Writing any content that describes Dutchwithjoost, compares to competitors, or makes claims about outcomes.

### curriculum.md
Explains how the Dutchwithjoost curriculum works at a teaching-design level. Contains:
- The two-phase lesson structure (practice → conversation)
- How practice sessions work (read/listen/write skills)
- How vocabulary progress and mastery work
- What "completing a lesson" means
- Why the system is designed this way

**Use this when:** Writing about what learners actually do in lessons, how progress works, or why the course structure differs from alternatives.

### feedback-coaching.md
Explains what feedback learners receive and when. Contains:
- Pronunciation feedback in Read & Speak and Listen & Speak
- Writing feedback that coaches rather than judges
- Conversation feedback that doesn't interrupt flow
- Progress and completion feedback
- Why feedback differs between practice and conversation

**Use this when:** Writing about how learners improve, what happens when they make mistakes, or how the app helps without being frustrating.

## For content creation

These documents provide the **specifics** that make content credible. Instead of writing "you get feedback on your pronunciation," you can write "you see which specific words need work, hear your own recording, and compare it to the correct pronunciation."

Specificity comes from these documents. Voice and framing come from `messaging.md` and `voice-guide.md` (in parent folder).

## Relationship to other strategy files

- `messaging.md` is authoritative for **what to say** about the product
- `curriculum.md` and `feedback-coaching.md` provide **the details** behind what messaging claims
- `voice-guide.md` (parent folder) governs **how to say it**
- `positioning.md` (parent folder) provides **competitive context**

When writing content, load the relevant product docs plus the appropriate strategy modules for your content type.
